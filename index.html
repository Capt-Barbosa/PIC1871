IRR — Internal Rate of Return
The annualised % return of an investment, accounting for the exact timing of every cash flow in and out. It is the true annual return rate. Example: IRR 15% means the investment grows at 15% per year.
Hurdle Rate — Preferred Return
The minimum annual return (typically ~8%) that must be delivered to LP investors before the GP can take any carried interest. It protects investors: if the fund underperforms, the GP earns nothing extra.
Carried Interest — The GP’s Profit Share
Once the hurdle rate is exceeded, the GP keeps 10–20% of all profits as carried interest. This aligns incentives: the GP only earns big if investors earn big first. PIC 1871 earns carry as GP co-owner (Associés).
